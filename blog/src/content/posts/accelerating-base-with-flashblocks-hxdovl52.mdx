noreferrer nofollow ugc" class="dont-break-out" href="https://docs.base.org/chain/flashblocks/apps#libraries"><u>which libraries support Flashblocks</u></a> in our docs.&nbsp;</p><h4 id="h-transaction-ordering" class="text-xl font-header">Transaction Ordering</h4><p>Flashblocks will change transaction ordering within blocks in two key ways:</p><p><strong>Timing</strong></p><p>Flashblocks are built every 200ms, each ordering a portion of the block. Unlike the current system where later-arriving transactions with higher priority fees can be placed at the top of the block, Flashblocks creates a time-based constraint. Once a Flashblock is built and broadcast, its transaction ordering is locked even if a transaction with a higher priority fee arrives later, it cannot be included in earlier, already built Flashblocks.</p><p><strong>High Gas Limits</strong></p><p>If your app creates transactions with large gas limits, we recommend monitoring to detect any changes in inclusion latency. Transactions with gas limits over 1/10 of the current block gas limit (currently 14 million gas), face additional constraints:</p><ul><li><p>Each Flashblock can only use a portion of the block's total gas limit</p></li><li><p>Flashblock 1: up to 1/10 of the total gas</p></li><li><p>Flashblock 2: up to 2/10 of the total gas</p></li><li><p>And so on for subsequent Flashblocks</p></li></ul><p>Consequently, transactions with large gas requirements must wait for later Flashblocks with sufficient gas capacity. For example, a transaction exceeding 1/10 of the block's gas limit cannot be included in Flashblock 1 and must wait for Flashblock 2 or later.</p><br><h4 id="h-understanding-reverts-reorgs-and-reliability" class="text-xl font-header">Understanding reverts, reorgs and reliability</h4><p><strong>Reorgs</strong></p><p>When the Flashblocks stream differs from the final block, there has been a reorg. This will result in some RPC responses returning data that could be different from the final block. In our initial release, this may happen. Although we expect it to be very infrequent, your application needs to be able to handle them. We plan to reduce how often these reorgs occur in future updates.</p><p><strong>Revert Protection</strong></p><p>In our initial release, revert protection will not be enabled. Transactions will continue to revert on Base as they do today. We plan to investigate enabling revert protection in future updates as part of our ongoing development roadmap.</p><p><strong>Reliability</strong></p><p>If there is a Flashblocks outage the chain will continue to progress, but fallback to the standard two second block times. During an outage:</p><ul><li><p>RPC calls and sending transactions will continue to work, with slower transaction inclusion</p></li><li><p>The websocket endpoint will not return any data<br></p></li></ul><p>Outages are expected to be infrequent - our SLA for Flashblocks uptime is 99.9%.</p><h3 id="h-node-operator-integrations" class="text-2xl font-header">Node Operator Integrations</h3><p>Flashblocks are an <em>optional</em> feature for your nodes. When not activated, your nodes will function exactly as they do today. Activating Flashblocks makes two changes to the execution client:</p><ol><li><p>They connect to the Flashblocks data stream</p></li><li><p>Flashblock information will be used to modify the responses of some ETH RPC endpoints</p></li></ol><h4 id="h-enabling-flashblocks" class="text-xl font-header">Enabling Flashblocks</h4><p>You'll need to run our <a target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow ugc" class="dont-break-out" href="https://github.com/base/node-reth"><u>Base Reth binary</u></a> with integrated Flashblocks support. If you're interested in seeing Flashblocks compatibility with other execution clients, please submit an issue on the <a target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow ugc" class="dont-break-out" href="https://github.com/base/node"><u>base/node</u></a> repository, and we'll explore expanding support.</p><p>For full configuration instructions and setup guidance, refer to our <a target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow ugc" class="dont-break-out" href="https://docs.base.org/chain/flashblocks/node-providers"><u>documentation</u></a>.</p><h4 id="h-receiving-flashblocks" class="text-xl font-header">Receiving Flashblocks</h4><p>As Flashblocks are built, they’re broadcast immediately by the <a target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow ugc" class="dont-break-out" href="https://specs.optimism.io/background.html?highlight=sequencer#sequencers"><u>sequencer</u></a> over a websocket. Our <a target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow ugc" class="dont-break-out" href="https://docs.base.org/chain/flashblocks/apps#websocket-api"><u>websocket endpoints</u></a> are publicly available but rate limited. If you operate a large number of nodes please reach out and we can provide you with an authenticated endpoint which will have minimal rate limiting.</p><h4 id="h-rpc-modifications" class="text-xl font-header">RPC Modifications</h4><p>Receiving a Flashblock, causes the nodes to update their internal in-memory state of the pending block, which is used to modify the responses to some ETH RPC’s.</p><p>If you have a caching or routing layer in front of your nodes, you may need to modify the behaviour for certain RPC endpoints. A full list of endpoints can be found <a target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow ugc" class="dont-break-out" href="https://docs.base.org/chain/flashblocks/apps#rpc-api"><u>here</u></a>.</p><h4 id="h-reliability" class="text-xl font-header">Reliability</h4><p>If there’s an outage of any part of the Flashblocks infrastructure nodes will continue to work as they do today with two second block times and blocks being distributed over the P2P network.</p><p>Our SLA for Flashblocks uptime is 99.9%. We’ve designed our infrastructure and node integration to gracefully handle Flashblock outages and fallback to the current situation of two second blocktimes.</p><h3 id="h-staying-in-the-loop" class="text-2xl font-header">Staying in the Loop</h3><p>We’re really excited to see what you build with Flashblocks.
